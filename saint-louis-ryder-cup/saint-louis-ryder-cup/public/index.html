🚩 House Rules

Know The Cup

⛳ The Golden Rule — Play Nice

Everything below is the default. Your group can deviate from any rule here — but both pairings must agree to it during the match. If you're ever bending a rule, get the opposing pairing's blessing first. No surprises, no funny business.

① Formats By Day

  • Friday — Scramble (both partners hit, play the best ball, repeat).
  • Saturday — Scramble.
  • Sunday — Shamble (both tee off, pick the best drive, then play your own ball in from there).

Captains set which format each group plays on Saturday & Sunday and pair up their own sides.

② Scoring — 21 Points Per Match

1 point per hole (tie = ½ each, no carryovers)18 Winning the front 9 (tie = ½ each)1 Winning the back 9 (tie = ½ each)1 Winner over the full 18 (tie = ½ each)1 Total per match21

③ Scramble — Lies & Order

  • Pick the better ball; both players play from there.
  • You may move the ball up to one club length, but you can't change the surface it's in. If the chosen ball is in the rough, both play from rough — same for a bunker, water, or woods / thick grass.
  • Within that surface, place the ball however you like — reasonably improving the lie is fair game. You just can't jump surfaces (rough → fairway, woods → rough, etc.).
  • Partners may play in either order.

④ Shamble — Lies & Order

  • Both tee off; the pair picks the drive they'll both play from, then each plays their own ball to the hole.
  • The second shot (from the chosen drive) follows the scramble rules above — one club length, same surface, either order, improve within the surface.
  • After that, on your own ball, it's play it as it lies — no fluffing, no improving.
  • Par 3s: everyone plays their own ball the whole hole — no choosing a tee shot.

⑤ The Hayden Handicap ＊

Hayden's the ringer, so the team playing against Hayden's group gets strokes — same idea all three days, just more of them in the shamble:

  • Friday & Saturday (scrambles): 1 stroke a side — on the hardest handicap hole of each nine (2 total).
  • Sunday (shamble): 2 strokes a side — one on each of the 2 hardest holes of every nine (4 total).
How a stroke works: on a stroke hole, subtract 1 from your group's score. Card a 5 with a stroke and it counts as a 4 — and since it's match play, that 4 beats Hayden's group if they make 5.

⑥ Gimmes

No automatic gimmes unless the group agrees to it before the match. Otherwise a putt isn't good until the ball is in the hole or your opponent concedes it.

⑦ Out of Bounds & Hazards

Red and white stakes work almost the same — the one difference is you can't play out of a white stake.

  • Red / yellow stakes (hazard): your call — play it as it lies with no penalty, or take a one-stroke penalty and drop where the ball crossed in (no closer to the hole).
  • White stakes (out of bounds): you cannot play it. Take a one-stroke penalty, then either drop where the ball crossed out (no closer to the hole) or replay from the spot of your previous shot.
  • Lost ball: one-stroke penalty, drop within two club lengths of where it was lost, no closer to the hole.
Bottom line: a white stake plays like a red stake — except you can't take the no-penalty "play it as it lies" option. With a white stake you must take the one-stroke penalty (drop where it crossed, or replay your last shot).

⑧ Winning The Cup

First team to 139 points takes the Cup.

252 points in play · reach 127 and it's clinched · finish 126–126 and the tiebreakers below decide it
Tiebreakers — if the teams finish level at 126–126, it's settled in this order:
  1. Total matchups won across the week.
  2. Largest margin of victory in a single matchup all week — tracked live on the site.
  3. Points won on Sunday.
  4. Winner of the final group on Sunday.
  5. Putt-off. A 5-v-5, penalty-kick-style putt-off from 3 paces out. Each team's five putters are 3 they pick themselves plus 2 of their players picked by the opposing team. Most made wins. Still level? Sudden death, round by round, with the 3 players who didn't putt the first time — until someone wins.
